The Digital Divide: Global Internet Access. Example: Netherlands vs Chad

An interactive story map exploring disparities in digital infrastructure and internet access.

Introduction

The digital divide refers to unequal access to information and communication technologies worldwide. This map compares internet penetration between two countries with contrasting digital access.

As we zoom in, notice how the colors on the map reveal stark differences in digital infrastructure.

Netherlands – High Internet Access

The Netherlands boasts near-universal internet coverage, supported by advanced broadband and mobile infrastructure, fostering broad digital inclusion and economic growth.

Explore the broadband coverage and population density layers to see how infrastructure supports high connectivity.

Chad – Limited Internet Access

Chad faces significant challenges with limited digital infrastructure, low population connectivity, and geographic barriers that restrict digital inclusion.

Toggle the terrain and mobile network layers to understand the barriers to digital access in Chad.

Conclusion & Initiatives

Bridging the digital divide requires coordinated efforts in infrastructure investment, policy development, and collaboration to make digital access affordable and equitable for all.
